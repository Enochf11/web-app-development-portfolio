Design Choices & Use of AI Tools

This page is a prototype Problem-Solving Page for the restaurant case study. As the fictional CTO of Don Pollo Grill, my goal was to show how an online menu and order estimator could reduce confusion about prices and make visits faster and clearer for customers and staff.

The layout is organised into sections (Mains, Sides, Drinks) with images, prices and quantity inputs. Customers can explore food options visually and calculate an estimated total before they arrive. In a real implementation this could connect to an online ordering or click & collect system.

I used generative AI tools (such as ChatGPT) to support my work by helping me refine page structure, CSS styling and JavaScript logic. I did not copy complete pages directly: instead, I adapted suggestions, combined them with my own ideas, and rewrote sections so that the final code reflects my understanding. All images and text are used as part of a student prototype, not a live commercial website.

How This Page Solves the Restaurant Scenario

This page is based on the restaurant problem-solving case study from the module. The brief was to design a simple online journey that lets customers explore the menu, choose items and see a clear estimated total before ordering.

  • Customer transparency: the prices are visible for every item and the estimated total updates based on the quantities entered.
  • Reducing queue time: customers could decide what they want and roughly how much it will cost before they reach the till.
  • Upselling and sides: the layout encourages customers to add sides and drinks, similar to real fast-casual restaurant menus.
  • Future data use: in a real system, the choices and totals could be stored and analysed to see which products are most popular, and when demand is highest.

This demonstrates how a basic web app can support a small restaurant with clearer information, better customer experience and the foundation for data-driven decisions.
